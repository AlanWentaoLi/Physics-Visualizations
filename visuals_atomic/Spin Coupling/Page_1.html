Spin-orbit interaction

For an electron in an atom, its angular momentum \( \mathbf{l} \) and spin \( \mathbf{s} \) couple to form a constant total angular momentum \( \mathbf{j} \). Before we apply the spin-orbit interaction, all the states described by the quantum numbers \( m_l \) and \( m_s \) are degenerate. However, in the presence of the pertubation, the energy levels now depend on the relative orientation of \( \mathbf{l} \) and \( \mathbf{s} \), so \( m_l \) and \( m_s \) are no longer good quantum numbers. Instead, the operator \( \mathbf{\hat{j}} \) gives a new quantum number \( j \), which takes values between \( l+s \) and \( |l-s| \) in steps of 1. Similarly, the operator \( \mathbf{\hat{j}}_z \) gives rise to a new quantum number \( m_j \). Hence, we obtain a set of operators $$\mathbf{s}^2\mathbf{\Psi}=\hbar^2 s(s+1)\mathbf{\Psi} \qquad \mathbf{s}_z\mathbf{\Psi}=\hbar m_s\mathbf{\Psi}$$ $$\mathbf{l}^2\mathbf{\Psi}=\hbar^2 l(l+1)\mathbf{\Psi} \qquad \mathbf{l}_z\mathbf{\Psi}=\hbar m_l\mathbf{\Psi}$$ $$\mathbf{j}^2\mathbf{\Psi}=\hbar^2 j(j+1)\mathbf{\Psi} \qquad \mathbf{j}_z\mathbf{\Psi}=\hbar m_j\mathbf{\Psi}$$ where
$$ m_s = -s,\dots ,s,$$ $$ m_l = -l,\dots ,l,$$ $$ m_j = -j,\dots ,j \text{ or } |l-s|,\dots ,|l+s| $$
