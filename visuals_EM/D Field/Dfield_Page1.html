Electric, Polarisation and D Fields in a Capacitor

A capacitor constructed from two parallel plates when considering the electric field will have a constant electric field between the two plates. The Electric field magnitude in the vacuum is given by \( E = V/d\), where the separation between plates \(d\), is much smaller than the plate size as to avoid edge effects. When a Dielectric material is introduced between the two plates the fields change. Inside the dielectric a Polarisation field will be created given by $$\mathbf{P} = \chi_{e}\varepsilon_{0}\mathbf{E}$$ The D Field, "electric displacement", is defined as $$\mathbf{D} = \varepsilon_{0}\mathbf{E} + \mathbf{P}$$
