The relative permittivity of a material is dependant on the angular frequency, \(\omega\), of the incident wave, given by $$\varepsilon_{r} = 1 - \frac{Nq^{2}}{\varepsilon_{0}m_{e}(\omega^{2}-\omega^{2}_{0}+i\omega\gamma)}$$ This can be related to the wavevecttor by \(k = n\omega/c\), this may be complex hence \(\mathbf{k}=(k_{R}+ik_I)\mathbf{\hat{z}}\) thus $$ e^{i(\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r}-t\omega)} = e^{i(k_{R}z - \omega t)}e^{-k_{I}z}$$ assuming low density matter (e.g Gas) we can binomal expand to gve the real and imaginary refractive index: $$ Re(n) = 1 - \frac{\omega^{2}_{d}(\omega^{2} - \omega^{2}_{0})}{(\omega^{2} - \omega^{2}_{0})^{2} + \omega^{2}\gamma^{2} }$$ $$ Im(n) = \frac{\omega^{2}_{d} \omega \gamma}{(\omega^{2} - \omega^{2}_{0})^{2} + \omega^{2}\gamma^{2}}$$
