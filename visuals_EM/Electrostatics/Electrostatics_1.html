The majority of classic electromagnetism is governed by only four equations, and the majority of static electromagnetism is governed by only two. These two equations are Gauss' law and Ampere's law, given by: $$ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_{0}}$$ and $$ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0$$
these laws are statements about the divergence of fields. Another way to think about field divergence is throught the movement of field lines. Positive divergence is equivilant to field lines moving out of a volume, whilst negative divergence is field lines moving inwards. Playing with the visualisation, you can see the number of field lines moving in/out of an arbitrary surface is proportional to the number of positive/negative charges held within the surface.
