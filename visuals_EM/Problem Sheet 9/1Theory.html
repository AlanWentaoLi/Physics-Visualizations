At normal incident from a vacuum (medium 1) to a dielectric (medium 2), the amplitude reflection coefficient and amplitude transmission coefficient are given by $$ r = \frac{1-\frac{Z_{1}}{Z_{2}}}{1+\frac{Z_{1}}{Z_{2}}} \quad \text{and} \quad t = \frac{2}{1+\frac{Z_{1}}{Z_{2}}}$$ respectively. Where \(Z\) is dependant on the refractive index \(n\) via $$ Z = \frac{1}{n}\sqrt{\frac{\mu_{0}}{\varepsilon_{0}}}$$ The Energy reflection coefficient of a wave is given by $$ R = \frac{{\mid E_{0}^{t} \mid}^2}{ {\mid E_{0}^{i} \mid}^2} = r^2 $$ and the Total Energy reflection coefficient is $$ T_{layer} = \frac{{\mid E_{total}^{t} \mid}^2}{ {\mid E_{0}^{i} \mid}^2} $$ The dependence of T on the thickness of the dielectric is calculated through $$ T_{layer} = {( 1 + \frac{4R}{{(1-R)}^2}\sin^2(k_{2}d))}^{-1}$$ Therefore when \(d\) is and integer multiple of \(\lambda/2\) where \(\lambda\) is the wavelength of the wave in the dielectric, then \(T_{layer} = 1\) for any value of \(R\). The visulisation shows the incident, reflected and transmitted waves.
