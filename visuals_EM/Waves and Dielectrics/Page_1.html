1. Boundary Conditions

Electromagnetic waves interacting within media is a problem that can be roughly solved by considering two fundamental phenomena. The first is boundary conditions; namely that the E and B fields must be continuous throughout all space, including at a dielectric boundary. These conditions are summed up via: $$\mathbf{E_{i}+E_{r}} = \mathbf{E_{t}} $$

$$\mathbf{B_{i}+B_{r}} = \mathbf{B_{t}} $$
