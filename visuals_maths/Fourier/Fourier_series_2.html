Triangular Fourier Series Derivation
$$ f(x)=\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \frac{-2A}{L}\cdot (x+L) \quad (-L < x < -L/2) \\ \frac{2A}{L}\cdot x \qquad \qquad (-L/2< x < L/2) \\ \frac{-2A}{L}\cdot (x-L) \quad (L/2< x < L) \end{array} \right. $$ $$ \text{f(x) is odd}$$ $$ \therefore a_n=0 $$ $$ \therefore b_n=\frac{2}{L}\int_0^L f(x)\cdot sin(\frac{n \pi x}{L})dx $$

$$ \text{For even n, the function is odd around L/2, so the function is zero.} $$ $$ b_n=\frac{2}{L}\int_0^{L} f(x)\cdot sin(\frac{n\pi x}{L}) dx = 0 \quad \text{for even n} $$ $$ \text{For odd n, the function is even around L/2, so the funciton doubles.}$$ $$ b_n=2\cdot\frac{2}{L}\int_0^{L/2} f(x)\cdot sin(\frac{n\pi x}{L}) dx \quad \text{for odd n} $$
