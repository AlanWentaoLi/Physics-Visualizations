Curl


The curl of a vector field, \(\mathbf{A}\) is: \[ \text{curl}\,\mathbf{A} = \nabla \times \mathbf{A} \] N.B. The curl can be thought of as a measure of "spinning per unit area".

For \(\mathbf{A} = (P,Q,R) \), we have the following definition: \[ \iint_D (\nabla \times \mathbf{A}) \cdot \mathbf{\hat{k}} \, dS = \iint_D \Big( \frac{\partial Q}{\partial x} (x,\,y)-\frac{\partial P}{\partial y}(x,\,y) \Big) \, dxdy \] This is known as microscopic circulation.
