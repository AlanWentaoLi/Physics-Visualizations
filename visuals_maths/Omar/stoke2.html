Stoke's Theorem:

Stoke's theorem relates the integral of the curl of a vector field, V, over an open surface S to the path integral of the vector field around a loop C defining the surface.

Mathematically, this can be written as $$ \iint_{S} \nabla \times \vec{V} \cdot \vec{dS} = \int_c \vec{V} \cdot \vec{dr}$$ Where dS is the surface normal vector and dr is the path tangent vector.
