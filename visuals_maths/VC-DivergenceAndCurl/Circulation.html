Circulation

Consider an arbitrary curve, \(C\),

  • Simple, piecewise-smooth and Closed,
  • Positive (Anti-Clockwise) Orientation,
  • \(D\) is an area enclosed by the curve, \(C\).
Then, for a vector field, \(\mathbf{A}\), the circulation, \(\Gamma\) is defined to be the line integral, \[ \Gamma = \oint_C \mathbf{A} \cdot \, d\mathbf{r}. \] Therefore, for \( \mathbf{A} = P(x,\,y)\mathbf{\hat{i}} + Q(x,\,y)\mathbf{\hat{j}}, \) the circulation, \(\Gamma\), is \[ \Gamma = \oint_C P(x,\;y(x))\;dx + Q(x(y),\;y)\;dy, \] where \(d\mathbf{r}=dx\mathbf{\hat{i}} + dy\mathbf{\hat{j}}\).
N.B. When integrating \(P\) and \(Q\), remember to convert \(P\) as a function of \(x\) only and \(Q\) as a function of \(y\) only.
Circulation is a measure of how much the vector field is "spinning" overall, it is called the macroscopic circulation
