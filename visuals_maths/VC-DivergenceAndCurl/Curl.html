Curl

The curl of a vector field, \(\mathbf{A}\) is: \[ \text{curl}\,\mathbf{A} = \nabla \times \mathbf{A} \] N.B. The curl can be thought of as a measure of "spinning per unit area".
Consider a 2D field \(\mathbf{A} = (P,Q,0) \), we have the following definition: \[ \iint_D (\nabla \times \mathbf{A}) \cdot \mathbf{\hat{k}} \, dS = \iint_D \Big( \frac{\partial Q}{\partial x} (x,\,y)-\frac{\partial P}{\partial y}(x,\,y) \Big) \, dxdy \] This is known as microscopic circulation.
The graph on the right hand side shows the spin of a small paddle wheel in two vector fields pointing to the opposite direction. The speed and the direction of its spin depends on the strength and direction of the field where it is placed.
